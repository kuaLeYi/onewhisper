×1: ARITHMETIC

Mathematics encompasses a growing variety and depth of subjects over history. Mental computations involving basic mathematical operations and methods will obviously provide a foundation for further study.
×2: NUMBER THEORY

Basic number theory is key because number theory has paved the way for the refinement of human knowledge on groups, rings, fields, Galois theory, algebraic geometry, stacks etc.
×3: PERCENTAGE

Percentages are a common way to represent relative magnitudes in everyday life. A sufficiently thorough introduction to percentage at a Primary 6 level preferably enables pupils to use it in work and recreation.
×4: RATIO

Ratios are a common way to represent relative magnitudes in everyday life. A sufficiently thorough introduction to ratio at a Primary 6 level preferably enables pupils to use it in work and recreation.
×5: SPEED AND RATE

The temporal rates studied here vary with time. Thus, they are mathematical functions and the questions generated promote the understanding of systematic change in quantity according to an independent variable. Here we consider the average and not instantaneous rate of change.
×6: BASIC ALGEBRA

Basic algebra is widespread in use and malleable in nature. The ability to understand and apply algebra gives one a base for higher mathematical education—and training in science and technology—along with certain skills appropriate for general decision-making.
×7: TRIANGLES & QUADRILATERALS

A motivation for the inclusion of the topic here is that many simple mathematical concepts regarding triangles and quadrilaterals are fit for use in various fields depending on the solver of the problem containing these concepts: the mathematical theory, being general, lends itself to diverse and specific practical situations.
×8: MEASUREMENT

The conceptual attainment Measurement requires will familiarise, as with the study of other geometric structures, students with the properties and language of typical polygons and polyhedra.
×9: MENSURATION AND CIRCULAR MEASURE

This topic aims to develop further an understanding of more essential geometric structures.

Some fixed formulae will be applied to 2D/3D shapes more difficult than in t8 and combinations thereof; eg cylinders, annuli and pyramids.
×10: AVERAGES

The need for the mean of any useful set of evenly distributed points can be encountered at any point in daily life. This topic is included because it allows for a basic, yet precise, gauge of the central tendency of such a set.

Considered here are word problems involving the averages of items in arithmetic progression and the averages of items not so.
×11: SUM AND PRODUCT

Complicated calculations are distinctly ubiquitous in modern Singaporean professional work and conceivably personal contemplation. This topic, which requires the knowledge of mathematical rules, the knowledge of truths about algebraic expressions and the logical observation of patterns and/or their potential for manipulation, may act as a prerequisite for the covering of seemingly unworkable mathematical tasks and undertakings involving addition, subtraction and multiplication only.
×12: SEQUENTIAL NUMBERS

Behind numerical sequences of straightforward and elementary progressions will lie, usually, straightforward and elementary relations/formulae. As such, quizzes containing related problems can improve students' general proficiency in recognising the pattern in and rapidly grasping some details about a sequence, including its sum, size and nth term.
×13: REMAINDERS AND MODULO

Remainders in division, along with the modulo operation, are always a valuable part of the repertoire of any solver of problems that require one to find some part left over with quotition or partition. With a grasp of this topic, students can analyse repeating blocks of numbers and unit digits well.
×14: ANGLES AND BASIC CIRCLE GEOMETRY

Angles and Basic Circular Geometry aims to explore approaches to mathematical figures that are made elegant with the introduction of propositions that can drastically simplify geometry work. Diagrams are encouraged on the solver's part but will not be provided.
×15: PLANAR AREA

Area plays an important role in mathematics today. The mathematical techniques discussed here will, being general, eventually be applicable to diverse and specific practical situations that involve area.
×16: TRIGONOMETRY

Trigonometry... is the soul of science. It is an eternal truth: it contains the mathematical demonstration of which man speaks, and the extent of its uses are unknown.
~Thomas Paine, The Age of Reason
Mathematical ideas:
Sin, cos and tan and variations
Relations of acute and obtuse angles
Unit circles and right-angled triangles
Sine and cosine rules
Word problems
×17: INTRODUCTORY COMBINATORICS

Combinatorics' problem-solving essence complements the theory-building essence underlying other aspects of mathematics. In actuality, it lends itself also to group and representation theory, statistics (studied later) and algorithms in programming, among others. This is mostly why the topic Introductory Combinatorics is included here.

'Combinatorics' part of qns:
Counting arguments for max and min solutions to word problems
Choosing vertices (on lines)
×18: INDICES

Not knowing about the laws of indices and not practising how to apply them contributes to certain weaknesses. By way of example, when indices are misused in fractions and negative numbers in formal mathematical examinations and if the use of scientific notation, which uses indices to express values concisely, is not very readily practicable by those who need it to be, the errors can be far-reaching. Conversely, this unit is meant to visit indices with breadth and depth.
×19: SETS

Sets' practical purpose does not exist in the way in which, for example, Fourier analysis has practical applications. To those who are not set theorists, the value of set theory is not in any particular theorem but in the language it gives us. With the specific knowledge that practices that are of of this unit will teach, one can unambiguously answer questions about various sets of objects.

Subordinate Matters: Descriptions | Membership including subsets and power sets | Cardinality | Special sets | Basic operations eg unions, intersections, complements and Cartesian products | Word problems
×20: INEQUALITIES

Inequalities will be a foundation for more advanced mathematical education about inequalities involving absolute values and inequalities involving rational functions. Discovering how to interpret inequalities is also a step towards learning how to solve them in everyday contexts.

Subordinate Matter: Compound linear equalities, with various types of notation used
×21: COORDINATE GEOMETRY

Coordinate geometry is chosen owing to the fact that it is widely used in physics, engineering, aviation, rocketry, extraterrestrial science and spaceflight, and is the grounds for most modern fields of geometry eg algebraic, differential, discrete and computational.

Subordinate Matters: Revision of gradient | Midpoints and linear distances | Area | Very simple proofs | Multiply and divide
×22: DIRECTION AND BEARING

This is, of course, highly applicable to navigation.

Subordinate Matters: Names and standard form | Relation to trigonometry | Word problems
×23: CONGRUENT AND SIMILAR

The recognition and use of congruent and similar shapes can expedite calculations and design. For instance, a decorative device may be put together by copying and manipulating just two shapes to produce versions of them of different sizes and in different positions. This unit considers the mathematical meaning of the terms 'similar' and 'congruent', which describe the relation between shapes like those in such a layout.

Subordinate Matters: Tests on triangles | Intercept and midpoint | Size
×24: GRAPHS AND (GRAPHS') EQUATIONS

Graphs and equations can relate data in abstract but meaningful ways.

Subordinate Matters: Gradient of equation, equation not graphed | Linear equations, graphs and solutions | Quadratic equations, graphs (intercepts, shape, extrema etc) and (possibly graphical) solutions (eg factorisation, quadratic formula and completing the square)
×25: ALGEBRA

Algebra is widespread in use and malleable in nature. The ability to understand and apply algebra gives one a base for higher mathematical education—and training in science and technology—along with certain skills appropriate for general decision-making.
×26: MATRICES

Matrices have not only compactness, but also numerous applications, both in mathematics and other sciences. The challenges involving them can serve to prepare the student of mathematics for their consideration and capable use in the present and future.
×27: SURDS

Surds come up when people create equations that model some behaviours so they can then be implemented in real life. They are needed to express and evaluate roots (radices) so that, indirectly, innovations such as 3-D videogames are possible (in this case through the normalisation of vectors to render solid geometric objects in a desired space).

Subordinate Matters: Indices: revision | Simplest surd form | Add and subtract | Multiplication and distribution | Rationalisation | Simplification | Equations of some numbers of steps
×28: CIRCULAR MEASURE

In this topic, the manipulation of circles and parts thereof, a skill vital for some mathematical education and some areas of work, is studied.
×29: CIRCULAR GEOMETRY

In this topic, the manipulation of circles and parts thereof, a skill vital for some mathematical education and some areas of work, is studied with questions that involve three theorems (namely: intersecting chords, tangent-secant and intersecting secants) and their application in forming short answers.
×30: 2-D VECTORS

2-D Vectors will provide a foundation for further study regarding advanced vector theory.

Subordinate Matters: Representations | Add and subtract | Multiply scalar and vector | Translation | Parallel vectors and collinearity
×31: STATISTICS AND ANALYTICS

This will assess the ability of the student to collect, organise, interpret, present and analyse data in ways that fit the general maths syllabus or curriculum, given that the ability to act on data is increasingly valuable both for groups and for individuals in today's intellectual society.
×32: PERMUTATION AND COMBINATION

The problem-solving essence of combinatorics complements the theory-building essence underlying other aspects of mathematics. In actuality, it lends itself also to group and representation theory, statistics (covered here too) and algorithms in programming, among others. This is mostly why the topic Permutation and Combination is included here.
×33: PROBABILITY

Probability is included because one uses probability in daily life to make decisions when one does not know the outcome with certainty.

Subordinate Matters: Complementary | Mutually exclusive, exhaustive | Independent | Listing and diagrams | Replacement and non-replacement
